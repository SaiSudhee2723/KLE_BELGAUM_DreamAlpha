MALARIA — NVBDCP / WHO Clinical Guidelines for India

CLINICAL FEATURES
Symptoms: Fever (high-grade, cyclical), rigors and chills, sweating, headache, body aches, nausea/vomiting, loss of appetite.
Hallmark: Fever paroxysms — cold stage (shivering), hot stage (high fever), sweating stage.
P. falciparum: more severe; may have continuous fever, altered sensorium, jaundice, bleeding.
P. vivax: periodic fever (48-hour cycle), anaemia, splenomegaly.

DIAGNOSIS
Mandatory: RDT (Rapid Diagnostic Test) OR thick blood smear microscopy.
RDT for P. falciparum detects HRP2 antigen.
Repeat smear if negative but suspicion high.
Supporting labs: Low platelet count, low haemoglobin, raised bilirubin.

TREATMENT — NVBDCP Protocol
P. vivax: Chloroquine 25 mg/kg over 3 days + Primaquine 0.25 mg/kg/day × 14 days (check G6PD).
P. falciparum: Artemisinin Combination Therapy (ACT) — Artemether-Lumefantrine (AL) or Artesunate-SP per state protocol × 3 days.
Severe malaria: IV Artesunate — refer immediately.
Avoid Primaquine in pregnancy and G6PD deficiency.

RED FLAGS (Refer to hospital immediately)
- Altered consciousness, seizures, coma
- Severe anaemia (Hb < 7 g/dL)
- Respiratory distress / pulmonary oedema
- Abnormal bleeding / haemoglobinuria (black urine)
- Jaundice with high parasite count
- Persistent vomiting preventing oral medication
- Hypoglycaemia (glucose < 50 mg/dL)
- Prostration (inability to sit/stand)

ASHA ACTION
1. Perform RDT immediately.
2. Positive P. vivax → start Chloroquine + Primaquine (check G6PD).
3. Positive P. falciparum → start ACT, refer to PHC same day.
4. Negative RDT + strong suspicion → refer for smear.
5. Record in HMIS.
6. Insecticide-treated bed-net distribution.
7. Report cluster (≥ 3 cases in one week) to BMO.

PREVENTION
Use insecticide-treated bed nets (ITN/LLIN).
Indoor residual spraying (IRS) per NVBDCP schedule.
Eliminate stagnant water breeding sites.
