ANAEMIA — ICMR / NHM India Guidelines

DEFINITION (ICMR Reference Values)
Haemoglobin (Hb) thresholds:
- Children 6 months–5 years: Normal ≥ 11.0, Mild 10–10.9, Moderate 7–9.9, Severe < 7 g/dL
- Children 5–11 years: Normal ≥ 11.5, Mild 11–11.4, Moderate 8–10.9, Severe < 8 g/dL
- Non-pregnant women: Normal ≥ 12.0, Mild 11–11.9, Moderate 8–10.9, Severe < 8 g/dL
- Pregnant women: Normal ≥ 11.0, Mild 10–10.9, Moderate 7–9.9, Severe < 7 g/dL
- Men: Normal ≥ 13.0, Mild 11–12.9, Moderate 8–10.9, Severe < 8 g/dL

CLINICAL FEATURES
Fatigue, weakness, pallor (conjunctival, palmar, nail-bed), dyspnoea on exertion, palpitations, dizziness, cold extremities, angular stomatitis, glossitis, koilonychia (spoon nails — iron deficiency), jaundice (haemolytic anaemia).
Severe: cardiac failure features, pedal oedema, tachycardia at rest, altered sensorium.

CAUSES IN INDIA
Iron Deficiency Anaemia (IDA): most common — poor diet, blood loss (worms, menorrhagia), malabsorption.
Nutritional: Folate/B12 deficiency → megaloblastic anaemia.
Haemolytic: Sickle cell, G6PD deficiency, malaria.
Chronic disease: TB, HIV, renal failure.
Pregnancy-related.

DIAGNOSIS
CBC: Hb, MCV (microcytic = iron deficiency; macrocytic = B12/folate), WBC, platelets.
Peripheral blood smear: hypochromic microcytic (IDA), oval macrocytes (B12/folate deficiency).
Serum ferritin (if available): < 12 ng/mL confirms iron deficiency.
Hb electrophoresis: for sickle cell, thalassaemia.
Stool for ova/cysts: hookworm infestation.

TREATMENT — NHM Protocol
IDA (Mild/Moderate): Iron + Folic Acid tablet daily.
Children: IFA syrup 1 mg/kg/day elemental iron × 100 days.
Pregnant women: 2 IFA tablets daily throughout pregnancy + 180 days postpartum.
Adolescent girls: Weekly IFA tablet under WIFS programme.
Severe anaemia: Blood transfusion if Hb < 7 g/dL (or < 8 in pregnancy).
B12 deficiency: Vitamin B12 IM injections.
Deworming: Albendazole 400 mg single dose (all school-age children, pregnant women after 1st trimester).

RED FLAGS
- Hb < 7 g/dL (Hb < 8 in pregnant woman) — refer for transfusion
- Signs of cardiac decompensation (oedema, tachycardia at rest)
- Altered sensorium
- Failure to respond to oral iron after 4 weeks

ASHA ACTION
1. Screen Hb using HemoCue or colour scale card.
2. Distribute IFA tablets under NHM programme.
3. Counsel on iron-rich diet (green leafy vegetables, jaggery, meat, pulses).
4. Administer Albendazole deworming per schedule.
5. Refer severe anaemia (Hb < 7) to PHC urgently.
6. Pregnant women: ensure daily IFA compliance.
7. Report severe anaemia in pregnancy immediately.
