JAUNDICE / HEPATITIS A & E — ICMR India Guidelines

CLINICAL FEATURES
Yellow discolouration of sclera (eyes) first, then skin.
Dark urine (tea/cola coloured), pale stools (clay-coloured).
Fatigue, loss of appetite (anorexia), nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain (right upper quadrant), low-grade fever.
Hepatitis A: Acute, self-limiting, spreads faecal-oral, more common in children.
Hepatitis E: More severe, especially dangerous in pregnancy (maternal mortality up to 25%).

GRADING JAUNDICE
Mild: Serum bilirubin 1–3 mg/dL.
Moderate: 3–10 mg/dL.
Severe: > 10 mg/dL (indicates serious liver dysfunction).

DIAGNOSIS
LFT: Raised AST/ALT (often 10× normal in hepatitis A/E), raised bilirubin (direct + indirect).
Hepatitis A: Anti-HAV IgM (acute infection).
Hepatitis E: Anti-HEV IgM.
Urine bilirubin: positive (urobilinogen).
Prothrombin time: prolonged in severe hepatitis (liver failure risk).
INR/PT > 1.5 = significant liver dysfunction.

TREATMENT
No specific antiviral for Hepatitis A or E.
Supportive: rest, adequate nutrition, oral hydration.
Avoid: alcohol, hepatotoxic drugs (Paracetamol in high dose, anti-TB drugs, herbal remedies).
Most cases resolve in 3–6 weeks.
Hepatitis E in Pregnancy: requires hospital admission (high risk of fulminant failure).

RED FLAGS (Refer urgently)
- Altered sensorium, confusion, hepatic encephalopathy
- Severe vomiting preventing oral intake
- Prolonged PT/INR > 1.5 (bleeding risk)
- Pregnancy with jaundice
- Bilirubin > 10 mg/dL
- Ascites (abdominal fluid)
- Spontaneous bleeding (gums, under skin)

ASHA ACTION
1. Suspect jaundice: yellow eyes + dark urine + fatigue.
2. Refer to PHC for LFT and hepatitis serology.
3. Counsel: rest, avoid heavy work.
4. Dietary advice: small frequent meals, avoid fatty/spicy food.
5. Strict hygiene: safe drinking water (boil/chlorinate), handwashing.
6. Pregnant women with jaundice: same-day referral to hospital — do not wait.
7. Investigate water source for community outbreak (HAV/HEV spread via contaminated water).
8. Report cluster (≥ 3 cases in community within 2 weeks) to BMO.
