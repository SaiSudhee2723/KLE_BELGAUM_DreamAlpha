PNEUMONIA — WHO IMCI / MoHFW India Guidelines

CLINICAL FEATURES
Cough with rapid breathing, chest indrawing, fever, difficulty breathing.
Children: fast breathing (≥ 60/min < 2 months; ≥ 50/min 2–12 months; ≥ 40/min 1–5 years), lower chest indrawing, nasal flaring, grunting.
Adults: productive cough, pleuritic chest pain, high fever (≥ 38.5°C), tachypnoea (RR > 20), reduced SpO2 (< 94%), dullness on percussion, bronchial breathing.

WHO IMCI CLASSIFICATION (Children)
Pneumonia: Fast breathing, no chest indrawing → oral Amoxicillin × 5 days.
Severe Pneumonia: Chest indrawing → refer urgently + pre-referral amoxicillin.
Very Severe Disease: Central cyanosis, unable to feed, altered sensorium → emergency referral + pre-referral antibiotics.

DIAGNOSIS
Clinical: rapid breathing + fever + cough = high suspicion.
SpO2 < 94% with room air = serious.
Chest X-ray: consolidation, lobar opacity.
CBC: raised WBC (bacterial), normal/low WBC (viral).
Sputum culture (hospital setting for severe cases).

TREATMENT
Mild (community): Amoxicillin 500 mg TID × 5 days (adults); Amoxicillin 40 mg/kg/day × 5 days (children).
Moderate: Add Azithromycin for atypical coverage.
Severe/Hospital: IV Ampicillin + IV Gentamicin (children); IV Ceftriaxone (adults).
Supportive: adequate hydration, paracetamol for fever, SpO2 monitoring.
Oxygen if SpO2 < 94%.

RED FLAGS (Emergency referral)
- SpO2 < 90% despite positioning
- Cyanosis (bluish lips/fingertips)
- Severe chest indrawing (children)
- Inability to drink/feed (children)
- Altered consciousness
- Age < 2 months with any danger sign
- Failure to improve after 48 hours of antibiotics

ASHA ACTION
1. Check RR (respiratory rate) in 1 full minute.
2. Check for chest indrawing and SpO2 (if oximeter available).
3. Children with fast breathing only → oral Amoxicillin + refer to PHC next day.
4. Any danger sign → immediate referral with pre-referral dose.
5. Adults: refer to PHC for evaluation if moderate/severe.
6. Ensure complete antibiotic course (do not stop early).
7. Vaccination: ensure PCV and Hib vaccines up to date (children).
