TYPHOID FEVER — ICMR India Guidelines

CLINICAL FEATURES
Week 1: Stepladder fever (rising each day to 39–40°C), headache, malaise, dry cough, constipation (adults) or diarrhoea (children).
Week 2: Sustained high fever, relative bradycardia (pulse lower than expected for fever), hepatosplenomegaly, rose spots (faint salmon-coloured spots on trunk — rare, seen in fair skin).
Week 3–4: Complications risk peaks — intestinal perforation, haemorrhage, encephalopathy.

DIAGNOSIS
Blood culture (gold standard, highest yield week 1): Salmonella Typhi or Paratyphi.
Widal test: H and O agglutinins — baseline titre varies by region; single titre ≥ 1:160 suspicious (serial rising titre more meaningful). Note: Widal has low specificity in endemic areas.
Typhidot IgM: rapid serology, positive from day 4–5.
CBC: leukopenia (low WBC), thrombocytopenia, mild anaemia.
LFT: mildly elevated transaminases.

TREATMENT
Drug of Choice: Azithromycin 500 mg OD × 7 days (uncomplicated, oral, outpatient).
Alternative: Cefixime 400 mg OD × 14 days.
For MDR Typhoid (Nalidixic acid resistant): Ceftriaxone IV or Azithromycin.
XDR Typhoid (resistant to ceftriaxone + azithromycin): Carbapenem — refer to tertiary hospital.
Fever relief: Paracetamol (avoid NSAIDs — risk of GI bleeding).
Steroids (Dexamethasone): ONLY for severe typhoid with altered sensorium at hospital.

RED FLAGS (Refer immediately)
- Intestinal perforation: sudden severe abdominal pain, guarding, rigidity, shock
- GI bleeding: blood per rectum, melaena
- Encephalopathy: altered sensorium, confusion
- Temperature > 40°C not responding to treatment
- Ileus (no bowel sounds, abdominal distension)
- Myocarditis/pericarditis

ASHA ACTION
1. Suspect: fever > 5 days + headache + no obvious focus.
2. Refer to PHC for blood test (blood culture / Widal / Typhidot).
3. Ensure strict hygiene while awaiting diagnosis.
4. Start Azithromycin only after confirmation (do not give empirically for every fever).
5. Ensure complete antibiotic course (risk of relapse if stopped early).
6. Hygiene counselling: safe drinking water (boil or chlorinate), hand washing before meals and after toilet, avoid raw foods/street food.
7. Typhoid conjugate vaccine (TCV): available for children 6 months–15 years under NIS.
