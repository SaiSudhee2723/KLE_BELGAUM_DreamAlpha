MATERNAL AND PRENATAL HEALTH — MoHFW India / WHO Guidelines

ANTENATAL CARE (ANC) SCHEDULE — NHM India
First visit: As early as possible (ideally within 12 weeks).
Second visit: 14–26 weeks.
Third visit: 28–34 weeks.
Fourth visit: 36 weeks onwards.
(Minimum 4 ANC visits per WHO; India recommends 8+ contacts under new ANC guidelines)

MINIMUM ANC PACKAGE (each visit)
- Weight and BMI
- Blood pressure measurement
- Abdominal examination (fundal height, foetal position/heart sounds from 20 weeks)
- Urine test (albumin, sugar)
- Haemoglobin (each trimester)
- Blood group and Rh factor (first visit)
- VDRL/RPR (syphilis screening, first visit)
- HIV test (PPTCT programme)
- Blood sugar (GDM screening, 24–28 weeks)
- Tetanus Toxoid (TT) immunisation × 2 doses (TT1 + TT2, or Td booster)
- Iron + Folic Acid: 2 tablets daily × minimum 180 days
- Calcium: 1g OD from 14 weeks
- Deworming: Albendazole 400 mg × 1 dose (after 1st trimester)

NORMAL VITALS IN PREGNANCY
BP: < 120/80 normal; ≥ 140/90 = gestational hypertension/pre-eclampsia.
Hb: ≥ 11.0 g/dL (anaemia < 11.0; severe anaemia < 7.0).
Weight gain: 11–14 kg total (underweight women need more).
Foetal heart rate: 120–160 bpm.

DANGER SIGNS IN PREGNANCY (Immediate referral)
- Vaginal bleeding at any stage
- Severe headache + blurred vision + oedema (pre-eclampsia)
- BP ≥ 140/90 mmHg
- Severe abdominal pain, uterine contractions < 37 weeks (preterm labour)
- Foetal movements reduced/absent
- High fever (possible infection/malaria)
- Difficulty breathing
- Convulsions (eclampsia)
- Passage of fluid/membranes before labour

ECLAMPSIA PROTOCOL (Life-threatening)
Magnesium Sulphate: Loading dose IV/IM (by nurse/doctor at facility) → immediate referral.
If convulsion at home → call 108, keep airway clear, lateral position, immediate transport.

POSTNATAL CARE (PNC)
First PNC: within 24 hours of delivery.
Second PNC: at 3 days.
Third PNC: at 7 days.
Fourth PNC: at 42 days.
Danger signs postpartum: Excessive bleeding (> 500 mL vaginal / > 1000 mL C-section), fever, foul-smelling discharge, breast engorgement, mastitis.

NEWBORN CARE
Breastfeeding: initiate within 1 hour of birth.
Colostrum: give (do not discard).
Kangaroo Mother Care: for low birth weight babies.
Eye care: erythromycin eye ointment.
Vitamin K injection.
BCG + OPV0 at birth.

ASHA ACTION
1. Register all pregnant women early (Mamta card / ANMOL).
2. Ensure minimum 4 ANC visits at PHC/subcentre.
3. Distribute IFA (180 tablets minimum) and calcium.
4. TT immunisation schedule.
5. Institutional delivery promotion (JSY incentive).
6. Post-delivery: first PNC visit within 24–48 hours.
7. Exclusive breastfeeding counselling.
8. Any danger sign → immediate referral, call 108.
