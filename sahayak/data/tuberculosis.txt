TUBERCULOSIS (TB) — RNTCP / NTEP India Guidelines

CLINICAL FEATURES
Pulmonary TB: Cough ≥ 2 weeks, blood in sputum (haemoptysis), fever (low-grade, evening), night sweats, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, chest pain.
Extrapulmonary TB: lymph node swelling (scrofula), pleural effusion, abdominal TB, TB meningitis, spinal TB (Pott's disease), TB of kidney/genitourinary.
High-risk groups: HIV/AIDS, diabetes, malnutrition, household contacts of TB patients, smokers, elderly.

DIAGNOSIS
Presumptive TB: cough ≥ 2 weeks + any one of: fever, night sweats, weight loss, haemoptysis.
CBNAAT/TrueNat: gold standard — also detects rifampicin resistance (DR-TB).
Sputum smear: AFB stain (less sensitive but widely available).
Chest X-ray: cavity, infiltrates, hilar lymphadenopathy.
Mantoux/TST: positive ≥ 10 mm (≥ 5 mm in HIV) — indicates exposure, not active disease.
Culture: drug sensitivity testing when needed.

TREATMENT — NTEP Regimen
DS-TB: 2HRZE + 4HR (6 months total). Daily fixed-dose combination (FDC).
H = Isoniazid, R = Rifampicin, Z = Pyrazinamide, E = Ethambutol.
DRTB/MDR-TB: Longer regimens (18–24 months) with second-line drugs — refer to DRTB centre.
Nikshay Poshan Yojana: ₹500/month nutritional support for all TB patients.
DOTS: directly observed treatment — ASHA supervises.

PREVENTIVE THERAPY (TPT)
Household contacts of sputum-positive TB (especially children < 5 years and HIV+ contacts): Isoniazid preventive therapy (IPT) 6 months.

RED FLAGS (refer urgently)
- Haemoptysis (blood in cough)
- Respiratory distress, severe breathlessness
- Altered sensorium (TB meningitis)
- Spinal pain/weakness (Pott's)
- HIV + presumptive TB

ASHA ACTION
1. Identify presumptive TB patients (cough ≥ 2 weeks).
2. Refer to NAAT testing centre (CBNAAT/TrueNat).
3. Register diagnosed patients on Nikshay portal.
4. Supervise DOTS daily dose.
5. Ensure ₹500/month Nikshay Poshan Yojana benefit.
6. Screen household contacts; refer children < 5 years for IPT.
7. Monthly follow-up; report treatment interruption immediately.
8. Contact tracing: test all household contacts.

PATIENT COUNSELLING
TB is curable with complete treatment.
Do not stop medicines even after feeling better.
Wear mask, ventilate room, cover mouth while coughing.
Balanced diet and adequate nutrition essential.
